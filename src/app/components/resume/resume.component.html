Career Profile

You can download a PDF version of my resume/CV here.

I've worked in the non-profit and for-profit sectors over the past eight years to gain a better understanding of how data can be used to inform effective practices across different sectors of society. My scientific background has allowed me to conduct climate change research in the U.S. and abroad, during which I became particularly interested in communicating statistical information to the general public. This led to my AmeriCorps service in Boston where I analyzed district-wide data from Boston Public Schools and collaborated with officials in an effort to lower high school dropout rates.

Currently, I work as a Senior Research and Data Associate at the Center for Court Innovation (CCI), a nonprofit focused on helping create a more effective and humane justice system. Each day, I work with data that has the capacity to give us insights into our judicial system, whether it be through monitoring housing court cases in Harlem or predicting the success of social programs on recidivism rates throughout the city - my goal is to utilize this information to make the process better and fairer for those involved and to help officials provide alternatives to jail when sentencing defendants for minor crimes.
